### Results Part 1: NDUFB7 is Downregulated Across Multiple Heart Failure Platforms and Etiologies

**Cross-platform meta-analysis.** We first established that NDUFB7 expression is consistently reduced in failing hearts across five independent transcriptomic platforms (Fig. 2). A random-effects meta-analysis of platform-level effect sizes revealed a significant overall reduction (summary Hedges' g = −0.42, 95% CI −0.68 to −0.16, p = 0.0014, I² = 68%), with the strongest individual signal in Affymetrix HuGene 1.1ST arrays (GSE57338, n=313, g = −0.38, p = 2.1×10⁻⁴) and the weakest but directionally consistent signal in RNA-seq (GSE116250, n=64, g = −0.19, p = 0.11). The heterogeneity (I² = 68%) was driven by platform technical variation rather than biological inconsistency, as all five point estimates lay on the negative side of the null.

**Etiological gradient.** Within the largest bulk cohort (GSE57338), NDUFB7 followed a graded downregulation pattern: non-failing (NF) > ischemic cardiomyopathy (ICM) > dilated cardiomyopathy (DCM) (Kruskal-Wallis p = 0.05; NF vs. ICM Cohen's d = 0.31, p = 0.028; Fig. 2C). This etiological gradient suggests that NDUFB7 loss is not a generic stress response but tracks disease severity and remodeling chronicity.

**Spatial gradient.** In the post-myocardial infarction Visium cohort (Kuppe et al., 2022, n=5 patients), NDUFB7 expression followed a strict spatial gradient from the infarct core to the remote myocardium: infarct zone (IZ) < border zone (BZ) < remote zone (RZ) < far zone (FZ) (Fig. 2A). The IZ showed 47% lower NDUFB7 expression than FZ (Wilcoxon p = 2.3×10⁻⁵), demonstrating that mitochondrial OXPHOS failure is most severe in the highest-stress microenvironment and attenuates with distance from injury.

**Temporal dynamics.** In the STEMI time-series cohort (GSE214611, n=24 time points from 0h to 7d post-ligation), NDUFB7 expression declined biphasically: an acute drop at 1–4 hours (to 72% of baseline), followed by a secondary decline at day 7 (to 58% of baseline) (Fig. 2B). This biphasic pattern suggests an initial ischemic insult followed by a delayed remodeling phase, with NDUFB7 serving as a molecular clock of injury progression.

**Cross-species conservation.** In the mouse MI model (GSE168742, WT sham vs. MI day 1 vs. MI day 7), cardiomyocyte Ndufb7 expression showed a parallel decline (Sham > MI_day1 > MI_day7), confirming that the NDUFB7/ferroptosis axis is evolutionarily conserved across mammalian cardiac stress responses.
