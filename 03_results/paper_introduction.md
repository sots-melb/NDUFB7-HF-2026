# Introduction

Mitochondrial dysfunction is a cornerstone of heart failure (HF) pathophysiology. The electron transport chain (ETC), particularly Complex I (NADH:ubiquinone oxidoreductase), suffers impaired activity in both ischemic and non-ischemic cardiomyopathies. However, most studies have examined Complex I as a holistic enzymatic entity, overlooking the distinct contributions of its 45 subunits. Among these, the nuclear-encoded accessory subunits—especially the 11-member B-subfamily—remain poorly characterized in human cardiac disease.

NDUFB7 (NADH dehydrogenase [ubiquinone] 1 beta subcomplex subunit 7) is a 137-amino acid accessory subunit located at the N-module/Q-module interface of Complex I. Unlike the core catalytic subunits (NDUFS1-3, ND1-6), accessory subunits are thought to stabilize the megacomplex structure and regulate supercomplex formation. Recent evidence from Leigh syndrome and mitochondrial disease suggests that even "non-catalytic" subunits can dictate Complex I assembly fidelity and tissue-specific vulnerability. Yet, whether individual accessory subunits exhibit spatially heterogeneous loss in human myocardial infarction (MI) remains unknown.

Spatial transcriptomics offers an unprecedented opportunity to map mitochondrial gene dynamics at tissue-architecture resolution. The Kuppe et al. (Nature 2022) human MI Visium dataset revealed zone-specific remodeling across infarct (IZ), border (BZ), and fibrotic (FZ) regions. We hypothesized that NDUFB7, given its small size and peripheral position within Complex I, might serve as a microenvironment-sensitive "sensor" of mitochondrial stress, exhibiting zone-specific depletion distinct from global transcriptional changes.

Here, we integrate spatial transcriptomics (Visium ST v1), four independent bulk transcriptomic platforms (Affymetrix and RNA-seq, total n=431), cardiac proteomics (PRIDE PXD010154), and tissue-specific eQTL analysis (eQTLGen and GTEx v11) to characterize NDUFB7 dynamics in human heart failure. Our findings reveal stable bulk expression but fibrotic-zone-specific spatial loss, positioning NDUFB7 as a peripheral, microenvironment-vulnerable component of the mitochondrial machinery.
